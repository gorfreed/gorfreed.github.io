Context Information

Imagine you are the Head of Localization and Narrative Design of a free-to-play gaming company called LOCALIZATION GAME TEST, based in beautiful Barcelona, with 300 employees. The firm generated over $90m in revenue during the last calendar year.
The Localization team consists of 3 Project Managers, 2 EN native copywriters and yourself. You report to the COO and have access to CEOs and other senior Managers.
The company has 3 live games requesting daily localization services, mainly for in-game content and liveops. The games are top 100 in RPG, City Builder, Resource Management categories on iOS and Android.


The company has several games in development phase but not requesting localization services yet. Imagine the company will publish a new game every 6 months.


Games are localized to 12 languages: EN, FR, DE, ES, IT, RU, TR, PT, KO, JP, CN, TW.


EN is the source language and ES localizations are done in-house. The rest of the languages are localized with an external vendor with gaming experience.


Other departments like Marketing are requesting localized texts but the volume is small compared to the games.


The localization process includes tools like the Style Guide, Glossary and Localization Memory. An internal tool called Localization Kit is used for the games and by copywriters to write the texts, this tool is synchronized with a TMS tool to manage the localization process, including Translation and Proofreading.

The total number of words to be localized from source in an average month is 14.000. This includes all localized languages and projects.


The company feels that Localization Quality is not the top variable to the success of a game, however it does want to provide an optimal game experience and therefore Localization has to be aligned with that.
